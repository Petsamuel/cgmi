Above Only Devotional

MONDAY AUGUST 24
Be Fruitful and Productive with the Word of God
Matthew 13:10-12 ( NKJV ), Luke 19:17

And the disciples came and said to Him, "Why do You speak to them in parables?" He answered and said to them, "Because it has been given to you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it has not been given. For whoever has, to him more will be given, and he will have abundance; but whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken away from him. *And he said unto him, Well done, thou good servant: because thou hast been faithful in a very little, have thou authority over ten cities.

Just as God expects us to be fruitful and productive with the resources and abilities that He has given to us, He also expects us to be fruitful and productive with His word. In our text above, after Jesus said that we have been given the ability to understand the mysteries of the kingdom, He went further to say, "For whosoever has, to him more will be given, and he will have abundance; but whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken away from him." Do you know that this was the same statement that the master who have his servants different talents also made? ( Matthew 25:29 ). What this statement means is that when you value and make good use of what you have, you will be promoted and given more. But when you despise and do not make good use of what you have, what you have will be taken away from you.

Just as the master who gave his servants talents expected them to be fruitful and productive with their talents, God also expects us to be fruitful and productive with His word that He send to us. God does not want us to hide and abandon His word as the servant with the one talent did. He wants us to absorb His word into our lives and allow His word produce great fruits and impact in our lives. He wants us to always go back to study , meditate, pray in line, declare and act upon His words. This attitude will make God's word produce great fruits in our lives. God's word will always prosper in what God has sent it for , Isaiah 55:11. Like it happened with the parable of the talents, we will give account for what we have done with God's word. As the servants who used their talents well were promoted and given more talents, when we make good use of God's word, we will grow and have more fruits in our lives.

Faith Declaration:

I value and make good use of God's word. As I study, meditate, pray, declare and act on God's word, great fruits will manifest in my life..

Church of God Mission International Incorporated was divinely born by the urge of the Holy Spirit with specific instructions of the Lord to Archbishop B.A. Idahosa as stated: “Raise up an army for me, to go with the gospel to all Nations. Gather together those whom I have called and I will send them with the fire of my spirit. Give my word and your example to many who will go to teach, preach and heal” Church of God Mission International, as it known today, grew from a very humble beginning in 1962 as a prayer group first named Calvary Fellowship at a shop on Ivbizua Street, off Mission Road. It was officially inaugurated in 1965 by Rev. Edgar Perkins. On October 26th 1968, Rev. Perkins approached the Assemblies of God to release Benson Idahosa, then a Sunday School Superintendent, to oversee the prayer ministry. The fellowship moved to No. 26, Forestry Road, Benin City with Pastor Benson Idahosa fully in-charge.
