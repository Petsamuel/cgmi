Above Only Daily Devotional
By: Archbishop M E Benson Idahosa

TUESDAY AUGUST 11
You Have Access to God and to His Power and Promises
John 16:26-27 (NLT ) , John 14:12 ( LB ), 2 Corinthians 1:20 (NIV)

Then you will ask in my name. I'm not saying I will ask the Father on your behalf, for the Father himself loves you dearly because you love me and believe that I came from God.
"... I tell you, anyone believing in me shall do the same miracles I have done , and even greater ones , because I am going to be with the Father. For no matter how many promises God has made , they are "Yes" in Christ. And so through him the "Amen" is spoken by us to the glory of God.

One of the privileges you enjoy as a believer is that you have direct access to God the Father. Ephesians 2:18 says, "For through him we both have access by one Spirit unto the Father." Christ is one with God and you are in Christ. This means that you are in God. The Bible says you are dead and your life is hid in Christ in God, Colossians 3:3. Because you are in Christ, you are always in God's presence.
Because you are always in God's presence, you can boldly talk directly with God and receive His mercy and grace anytime you want and anywhere you are, Hebrews 4:16. You do not need any human intermediary. There is no man or woman that gives you access to God or that has more access to God than you. Jesus has given you the very same access to God that He has and He has also given you His authority. This is why you can pray with boldness and expect the same answers and results that Jesus Himself will get. In John 17:22 , Jesus said He has given us His glory and in verse 23, He said that God the Father loves us just the same way He loves Him. It is not something we achieved on our own. God gave us this right and privilege through Christ. So we do not boast in ourselves or in any man or woman, we boast in Christ.

By His Spirit, Christ has also given us access to His very same power and ability , Acts 1:8. We can now represent Him and function like Him in every single way. This is why Apostle Paul said, "I can do all things through Christ that strengthens me," Philippians 4:13 . He was also very confident of God's provision for all his needs, Philippians 4:19. Nothing is impossible for you. Because of Jesus, you also have a right to ALL the promises of God. They are already your reality. As you accept and declare them as your reality, you will experience their manifestation in your life.
―Prayer:
I thank you Dear Father , for the great love you have for me and for the access you have given me to your presence, power and promises.

SUNDAY SCHOOL LESSON
DATE: SUNDAY AUGUST 09, 2020 TOPIC: THE BELIVERS' CONDUCT TEXT: TITUS 2:1-15 CENTRAL THOUGHT : Grace projects and promotes Godliness, right living and Good work.

INTRODUCTION: In today's world, there seems to be a silent resentment between the aged and the young ones of the society as a result of conflict of ideas. The church has not been immune to this because there are tendencies that portray the young as being in hurry to do away with the old school and to carve a niche for themselves. Having people of different ages and means in church is supposed to make the church strong, balanced and complete. However, there are imminent challenges here and there. The role of an efficient leadership is to harness Such diversities to the advantage of the church.

Grace is the common denominator for salvation among all believers. It is open to all, and all are invited to partake of her benefits. To love and live in sin is to trample under foot the works of grace perfected by the blood of Jesus Christ. Salvation through grace should be the beginning of practical demonstration of God's nature by any believer. In this study, we shall explain the nature and purpose of God's grace, and how it should motivate in us a life of service and obedience to God.
