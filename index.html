Above Only Daily Devotional

By: Archbishop M E Benson Idahosa
Thursday, August 15.
Grow in Your Sonship Consciousness
Galatians 4:6-7 (LB), Romans 8:16 (LB & AMP), Psalms 82:6 (NIV).

And because we are his sons, God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, so now we can rightly speak of God as our dear Father. Now we are no longer slaves but God’s own sons. And since we are his sons, everything he has belongs to us, for that is the way God planned. For his Holy Spirit speaks to us deep in our hearts and tells us that we really are God's children. The Spirit Himself [thus] testifies together with our own spirit, [assuring us] that we are children of God. “I said, ‘You are “gods”; you are all sons of the Most High.

The Jews had a deep understanding of what it means to be called a son of God. That is why when Jesus called God His Father, the Bible says the Jews become more determined to kill Jesus because by calling God His Father, He was making Himself equal with God, John 5:17-18. By our faith in Jesus, we have become sons of God like Jesus, John 1:12. Jesus is no more the only begotten son of God. He is now the first born among many brethren, Rom. 8:29. The Bible describes us as the church of the first born, Heb. 12:23. Our first text above says we are no longer slaves of God. We are sons of God and God has put the Spirit of His Son Jesus into our hearts. Now, God wants us to relate with Him just as Jesus His Son relates with Him. The Bible says we have been accepted in the beloved, Eph. 1:6. This means that we are God’s beloved sons and daughters just like Jesus is. When we are conscious that God is our loving Heavenly Father, there is an activation of both His life, love and abundance in our lives. We are members of God’s household and as God expects a man to take care of His household, God will also take good care of us.

Trying to relate with God and earn His favour by keeping the law is what makes us slaves or servants. But putting our faith in Jesus and receiving God’s abundant love and grace is what makes us God's sons. Slaves do not have rights and privileges. Slaves do not enjoy any inheritance. Slaves do not enjoy a loving relationship with their masters. In John 8:35 (GNB), Jesus said, “A slave does not belong to a family permanently, but a son belongs there forever. " One of the assignments of the Holy Spirit in our lives is to strengthen our conviction that we are sons of God. He spreads God’s love in our hearts, Rom. 5:5.

Prayer Focus: Rejoice and thank God for making you His son or daughter. Ask Him to help you grow in your consciousness that He is your Father. Download

SUNDAY SCHOOL LESSON
DATE: SUNDAY AUGUST 09, 2020 (Last Week)
TOPIC: THE BELIVERS' CONDUCT TEXT: TITUS 2:1-15
CENTRAL THOUGHT :Grace projects and promotes Godliness, right living and Good work.

INTRODUCTION:In today's world, there seems to be a silent resentment between the aged and the young ones of the society as aresult of conflict of ideas. The church has not been immune to this because there are tendencies that portray the young as being in hurry to do away with the old school and to carve a niche for themselves. Having people ofdifferent ages and means in church is supposed to makethe church strong, balanced and complete. However, thereare imminent challenges here and there. The role of an efficient leadership is to harness Such diversities to the advantage of the church.

Grace is the commondenominator for salvation among all believers. It is opento all, and all are invited to partake of her benefits. To love and live in sin is totrample under foot the worksof grace perfected by theblood of Jesus Christ. Salvation through graceshould be the beginning of practical demonstration of God's nature by any believer.In this study, we shall explain the nature and purpose of God's grace, and how it should motivate in us a life ofservice and obedience to God.
