Above Only Daily Devotional

By: Archbishop M E Benson Idahosa
WEDNESDAY AUGUST 12
You Have a Right to God's Leading and Direction
Psalms 23:1-3, John 15:15-16

The Lord is my Shepherd; I shall not want. ... he leadeth me beside the still waters , ... He leadeth me in the paths of righteousness for his name's sake. I do not call you servants any longer, because servants do not know what their is doing. Instead, I call you friends, because I have told you everything I heard from my Father. You did not choose me; I chose you and appointed you to go and bear much fruit, the kind of fruit that endures. And so the Father will give whatever you ask of him in my name.

One of your divine rights as a child of God is that you have access to the mind and voice of God. You have the ability to receive divine leading and direction from God and the ability to understand the things of God. Jesus said His sheep hears His voice, John 10:27. Peter once asked Jesus why He spoke to the public in parables but He came back in secret to explain those parables to them His disciples and Jesus said, "...it is given unto you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it is not given," Matthew 13:11b. As a believer, the things of God are no longer mysteries to you. When the Bible talks about the hidden wisdom of God that is for your glory, ( 1 Corinthians 2:7 ) it does not mean that God hides His wisdom from you as a believer. God hides His wisdom from the world but He shows it to you.

One of the reasons for Jesus's fruitfulness when He was on earth, was that He always knew and aligned Himself with what God the Father was saying and doing, ( John 5:19 , John 8:28 ). Right now, you also have this same ability. When ever you need the Wisdom of God in any area of your life, just ask God for it and He will give it to you liberally or abundantly, James 1:5. God will not withhold any good thing from you, Psalms 84:11. There is nothing that God cannot reveal to you.

When Daniel needed the dream of Nebuchadnezzar to be revealed to him, in His mercy, God revealed it to him and many lives were preserved, Daniel 2:17-20. You have received the Spirit of God so that you can know the deep things of God, 1 Corinthians 2:9-12. By the unction of the Holy Spirit you can know all things, 1 John 2:20 & 27. You will divinely know what the issue really is and know the right step or action to take and you will succeed. Download

SUNDAY SCHOOL LESSON
DATE: SUNDAY AUGUST 09, 2020 (Last Week)
TOPIC: THE BELIVERS' CONDUCT TEXT: TITUS 2:1-15
CENTRAL THOUGHT :Grace projects and promotes Godliness, right living and Good work.

INTRODUCTION:In today's world, there seems to be a silent resentment between the aged and the young ones of the society as aresult of conflict of ideas. The church has not been immune to this because there are tendencies that portray the young as being in hurry to do away with the old school and to carve a niche for themselves. Having people ofdifferent ages and means in church is supposed to makethe church strong, balanced and complete. However, thereare imminent challenges here and there. The role of an efficient leadership is to harness Such diversities to the advantage of the church.

Grace is the commondenominator for salvation among all believers. It is opento all, and all are invited to partake of her benefits. To love and live in sin is totrample under foot the worksof grace perfected by theblood of Jesus Christ. Salvation through graceshould be the beginning of practical demonstration of God's nature by any believer.In this study, we shall explain the nature and purpose of God's grace, and how it should motivate in us a life ofservice and obedience to God.
