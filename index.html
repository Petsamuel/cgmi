Above Only Daily Devotional

By: Archbishop M E Benson Idahosa
Thursday, August 13
Divine Preservation and Transformation
Lamentations 3:21-24, Psalms 107:1, Psalms 103:12-14

This I recall to my mind, therefore have I hope. It is of the LORD'S mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassion fail not. They are new every morning: great is thy faithfulness. The LORD is my portion, therefore will I hope in him. O give thanks unto the LORD, for he is good: for his mercy endureth for ever. He has removed our sins as far away from us as the east is from the west. He is like a father to us,tender and sympathetic to those who reverence him. For he knoweth our frame; he remembereth that we are dust.

God is rich in mercy (Eph. 2:4) and He manifests His mercies in our lives in various ways. The mercy of God keeps and preserves us in different ways. It is not our beauty, smartness, experience, carefulness, connections or anything else. The Bible says, “No king is saved by the great size and power of his army; a mighty man is not delivered by [his] much strength. A horse is devoid of value for victory; neither does he deliver any by his great power. Behold, the Lord’s eye is upon those who fear Him [who revere and worship Him with awe], who wait for Him and hope in His mercy and loving-kindness," Psa. 33:16-18 (AMP). God wants us to reverence Him and put our hope in His mercy and in nothing else, Psa.147:10-11. Every effort of man to build something or protect himself is in vain, except God provides His help, Psa. 127:1.

When the prodigal son was returning home, he was not sure if his father would even accept him as a servant but the father sighted him from afar, ran to him, embraced him and kissed him. The prodigal son might have been attacked and lynched because of the shame he brought to his community, but by running to him and embracing him in love, the father saved and preserved him, Luke 15:11-24. Jesus similarly miraculously preserved the life of the woman caught in adultery from the death she deserved at the hands of her accusers, John 8:3-11. Now, do you think this prodigal son or the woman caught in adultery would like to go back to their wayward life? The mercy they received will not only preserve their lives but also change their lives. When we truly understand the mercy of God, it will motivate us to love Him more and serve Him wholeheartedly. The Bible says the goodness of God leads to repentance, Rom.2:4.

Prayer: My dear Father, I thank you for how you have been preserving my life by your mercy. I yield myself to you Lord. My hope is in your mercy.. Download

SUNDAY SCHOOL LESSON
DATE: SUNDAY AUGUST 09, 2020 (Last Week)
TOPIC: THE BELIVERS' CONDUCT TEXT: TITUS 2:1-15
CENTRAL THOUGHT :Grace projects and promotes Godliness, right living and Good work.

INTRODUCTION:In today's world, there seems to be a silent resentment between the aged and the young ones of the society as aresult of conflict of ideas. The church has not been immune to this because there are tendencies that portray the young as being in hurry to do away with the old school and to carve a niche for themselves. Having people ofdifferent ages and means in church is supposed to makethe church strong, balanced and complete. However, thereare imminent challenges here and there. The role of an efficient leadership is to harness Such diversities to the advantage of the church.

Grace is the commondenominator for salvation among all believers. It is opento all, and all are invited to partake of her benefits. To love and live in sin is totrample under foot the worksof grace perfected by theblood of Jesus Christ. Salvation through graceshould be the beginning of practical demonstration of God's nature by any believer.In this study, we shall explain the nature and purpose of God's grace, and how it should motivate in us a life ofservice and obedience to God.
